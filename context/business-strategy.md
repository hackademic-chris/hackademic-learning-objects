# Business Strategy Context

## Company

Hackademic Solutions

## Business Type

AI security advisory and training firm.

## Current Stage

Startup mode.

The company is building training content to support customer acquisition, revenue generation, advisory services, and eventual productization.

## Primary Business Goals

Training content should help:

- build visibility
- create LinkedIn engagement
- attract potential clients
- support low-cost paid training
- create advisory conversations
- create reusable client-facing assets
- establish credibility in AI security and Microsoft AI adoption

## First Content Strategy

The first content experiments should use low-risk foundation topics.

These are useful because they are easier to test, easier to publish, and helpful for validating the learning-object production agent.

Initial foundation topics may include:

- JSON for AI Security
- YAML for Governance and Automation
- Git for Accountability in AI Workflows
- Markdown for AI Workflows
- APIs and Connectors for AI Tools

These topics are not the strategic endpoint.

They are calibration content.

They help test the agent, voice, structure, components, review process, and workflow.

## Strategic Training Pathway

The first serious pathway is:

Secure Microsoft AI Adoption for Mid-Sized Organizations

This pathway should help mid-sized organizations adopt AI in the Microsoft ecosystem without creating unmanaged security, governance, data, identity, or operational risk.

## Strategic Microsoft Focus

The strategic Microsoft pathway should include:

- Microsoft Cloud Adoption Framework
- Azure Well-Architected Framework
- Microsoft AI adoption planning
- identity and access risk
- data governance and oversharing
- security operations implications
- AI governance and risk management
- Microsoft security and governance stack
- executive and leadership decision-making

## Audience

Primary audiences include:

- C-level executives
- senior leaders
- IT leaders
- cybersecurity leaders
- governance, risk, and compliance stakeholders
- security managers
- AI-curious professionals
- practitioners who need technical literacy foundations

## First Paid Offer

The first paid offer will likely be a low-cost Skool course unless advisory work arrives first.

Training should be designed so it can support advisory conversion.

The end of a course should point toward a practical next step, such as:

- readiness assessment
- workshop
- governance review
- Microsoft AI adoption advisory session
- secure AI adoption checklist
- risk review
- implementation roadmap

## Advisory Conversion

Learning objects should be designed to produce artifacts that can lead to advisory work.

Useful artifacts include:

- readiness worksheets
- risk registers
- use-case intake forms
- governance checklists
- executive decision worksheets
- policy review guides
- secure AI adoption roadmaps
- framework mapping exercises

## Business Rule

Do not build a large platform before validating demand.

Build reusable componentized learning objects first.

Use the learning objects to create content, courses, client conversations, and advisory assets.

Do not confuse infrastructure work with revenue progress.
