# Brand Voice

## Brand

Hackademic Solutions

## Voice Summary

Hackademic Solutions combines practical cybersecurity experience, academic rigor, research-backed thinking, operational fluency, and plainspoken advisory guidance.

The tone should be useful, credible, and direct.

The content should feel like it comes from someone who has taught the material, worked in the field, and understands organizational reality.

## Voice Attributes

Use a voice that is:

- practical
- academically grounded
- clear
- skeptical
- useful
- operationally fluent
- plainspoken
- experienced
- credible for executives
- accessible to managers
- respectful of technical practitioners

## Style

Prefer:

- concrete examples
- practical decision points
- short explanations of complex ideas
- grounded skepticism
- useful frameworks
- plain language
- thoughtful analogies
- applied scenarios
- clear next actions

Avoid:

- generic corporate training filler
- AI hype
- motivational fluff
- vague transformation language
- empty innovation language
- overused business clichés
- exaggerated claims
- unsupported compliance claims
- overly academic prose
- childish simplification
- jargon without explanation
- unnecessary technical jargon

## Dry Humor

Dry humor is acceptable when it clarifies a point or makes the content more human.

Use it sparingly.

Do not let humor undercut credibility.

Good style:

"SCORM is useful. It is not where pedagogical ambition goes to stretch its legs."

"Do not build a dashboard for learners you do not have yet."

Bad style:

Forced jokes, sarcasm that insults the learner, or humor that distracts from the lesson.

## Audience Handling

For executives:

- focus on decisions, risk, governance, accountability, cost, and organizational readiness
- avoid technical rabbit holes
- include practical questions leaders should ask

For managers:

- focus on implementation, supervision, policy, workflow, and team behavior
- include checklists, examples, and scenarios

For practitioners:

- include concrete workflows, technical examples, labs, tools, and artifacts

For foundation learners:

- explain technical concepts without assuming developer background
- connect concepts to AI security, automation, governance, and business decisions

## Brand Promise

Translate complex AI security and cybersecurity concepts into practical learning assets that help organizations make better decisions and reduce avoidable risk.
